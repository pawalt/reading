## Overview

The Training docs make this spike narrower and more practical than a generic survey of disaggregated serving.
April planning says the team wants an example of **autoscaling, disaggregated inference for RL**. The same
plan also calls out a customer ask for **Modal-managed inference with training on Tinker**, with SGLang
coordination named as the main learning target. The Monarch note pushes in the same direction, arguing that
rollout and training separation is a good fit for Modal’s capacity model if weight movement can be made cheap.

Disaggregated serving first became popular because prefill and decode want different resources. RL post-training
pushed the same architectural instinct further: keep rollout and training separate so each cluster can specialize.
The catch is that specialization creates a synchronization boundary. If updated weights cannot move across that
boundary cheaply, the architecture becomes unstable or too slow to matter.

That is why delta compression matters here. In the team docs it is not an abstract optimization; it is the
difference between a nice architecture diagram and a practical Modal example. The most useful reading on this
topic now comes from systems teams who had to make disaggregated training and inference work in production.

The newest public example is Cursor's Composer 2 stack. The technical report is useful because it makes the
weight-sync path explicit: asynchronous RL across multiple regions, deltas uploaded every training step, pipelined
compression and hotload, and geographically distributed inference clusters reconstructing weights from a shared
delta chain. Fireworks' companion post is useful for the opposite reason: it frames the same pattern as an
operational product surface rather than a one-off research hack.

## Training team context

- April planning says this operating model fits **Modal’s capacity model** and is worth chasing directly.
- The team wants to publish an example of **delta-compressed weight updates** in the multinode training guide.
- The Perplexity/Tinker item is explicitly described as a learning vehicle for **SGLang coordination**.
- The Monarch note makes the same architectural bet from another angle: disaggregate the training cluster from
  the rollout pool, then use better synchronization and orchestration to keep the split economical.

## Key themes

- Disaggregation improves utilization only if **state transfer** stops being the dominant cost.
- In the team’s case, the concrete target is not generic serving throughput but **RL rollout/training separation on Modal**.
- Delta compression is attractive because RL updates are often structured and sparse relative to full weight snapshots.
- The winning systems pair compression with **point-to-point transfer**, pipelining, and static scheduling.
- The freshest public systems evidence comes from **Cursor + Fireworks**, which shows this pattern surviving outside a research prototype.
- The real operational question is not just speed, but whether weight sync can stay fast enough to avoid train-infer drift while keeping rollout pools warm and autoscalable.

## Suggested reading order

1. Start with **Disaggregated Inference: 18 Months Later** for the architectural frame the team is betting on.
2. Read **Disaggregated Prefill and Decode** to understand the concrete serving split before adding RL-specific weight sync.
3. Read **Composer 2 Technical Report** for the clearest new public example of multi-region RL inference with delta-compressed checkpoint sync.
4. Read **Scaling and Optimizing Frontier Model Training** to see how Fireworks productizes the trainer and deployment side around that loop.
5. Read **Weight Transfer for RL Post-Training in under 2 seconds** as the closest public analogue to the team’s Perplexity/Tinker workstream.
6. Read **RDMA Point-to-Point Communication for LLM Systems** for the transport layer ideas that make the previous step reusable.
7. Finish with **PULSE** to evaluate whether sparse delta-style synchronization can be the next optimization once the basic disaggregated path is working.
